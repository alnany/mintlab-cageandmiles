How to File for Divorce in Los Angeles

Filing for divorce in Los Angeles involves meeting California’s legal requirements and complying with Los Angeles County court procedures. One spouse begins the process by filing a petition and serving the other party with the required legal documents.

California law also imposes Automatic Temporary Restraining Orders ( ATROs ) at the start of most divorce cases, which are designed to preserve the marital estate and maintain stability during the proceedings. Understanding these early steps clearly helps clients avoid delays and unnecessary conflict, ensuring their cases progress efficiently and strategically.

What Happens After You File for Divorce

After the initial filing and service, both parties are required to exchange financial disclosures outlining income, expenses, assets, and debts. This stage is often one of the most time-consuming and critical parts of the divorce process.

Once disclosures are complete, spouses may begin negotiating issues such as custody , support , and property division . Some cases resolve through settlement or mediation, while others require court involvement to reach a final resolution. Experienced legal guidance during this phase ensures negotiations remain informed, organized, and aligned with your long-term interests and goals. We stand by our clients throughout this process to protect their rights and advocate for their best outcomes.

How should I choose a divorce attorney in Los Angeles?

Choosing a divorce attorney is a personal process. When searching for an attorney, look for a firm that has expertise in family law and one that is specialized in your geographic area. Cage & Miles are family law experts in California, and our attorneys have extensive knowledge and experience handling divorces in Los Angeles. Is it hard to file for divorce in Los Angeles?

Filing for divorce is a multi-step, months-long process. Working with a seasoned attorney who has a deep understanding of the law and experience in the local courts is vital to ensuring smooth divorce proceedings. Expert Los Angeles divorce attorneys, like those at Cage & Miles, can help clients more easily navigate the nuances and intricacies of the divorce process.

Do you have to have an attorney for a divorce in California?

Though a divorce lawyer is not required for an uncontested divorce in California, we recommend seeking the advice of an experienced Los Angeles divorce lawyer. This helps ensure your assets remain protected and that community assets are divided equitably , especially when one or both parties are high-net-worth individuals.

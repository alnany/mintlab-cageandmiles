An experienced Riverside military divorce attorney can help ensure these protections are applied appropriately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to hire a divorce lawyer?

Though it’s possible to file for divorce without an attorney, it’s a complicated and time-consuming process. Hiring an experienced family law attorney provides legal guidance to protect your interests and ensure every step is completed properly. At Cage & Miles, our attorneys are seasoned in handling Riverside County divorce cases, and they understand all local rules and regulations, are familiar with other attorneys and judges, and will work hard to help you achieve your desired outcome.

Will my divorce proceedings go to trial?

Not all divorce cases go to trial. The mediation process allows both parties to come to a settlement outside the courtroom with the help of neutral, third-party advisors like attorneys or mediators. Mediation is less expensive and often faster than trial, making it a popular option for the resolution of divorce. Cage & Miles’ lawyers are experienced mediators, allowing them to facilitate an agreement between both parties. They can also act as your attorney to help guide you throughout the mediation process, keeping your best interests in mind.

What should I consider when choosing my divorce attorney?

When choosing a Riverside County-based family law attorney, it’s important to find a professional who will support your interests both inside and outside of the courtroom. Divorce can be stressful, and the right professional will provide added guidance to ensure you have less to worry about.

Family law is a complicated field, so it’s best to choose an attorney who has experience handling similar cases. Once you have narrowed your search, you should schedule a consultation with the attorney to ask any detailed questions you may have about their level of experience and their communication style. Attorneys like those at Cage & Miles put your priorities first to ensure you feel supported and informed through every step of the process.

How long does a divorce take in Riverside County?

California requires a minimum six-month waiting period from the date divorce papers are served. In Riverside County, the total timeline depends on factors such as child custody disputes, property division, and whether the case settles or proceeds to trial. A Riverside divorce attorney can help keep the process moving efficiently.

Is divorce mediation confidential in Riverside County?

Mediation confidentiality can vary depending on whether mediation is private or court-connected. Speaking with a Riverside family law attorney before mediation helps you understand how information may be used and how to best protect your interests.

What should I bring to a consultation with a Riverside family law attorney?

It’s helpful to bring financial documents, court paperwork, prenuptial or postnuptial agreements, and a list of questions or concerns. This allows your Riverside family law attorney to provide more tailored guidance from the start.

Where are family law cases heard in Riverside County?

Divorce and family law matters are typically handled at the Riverside Historic Courthouse, the Menifee Justice Center, or the Larson Justice Center in Indio. The specific location depends on your zip code of residence.

Does Riverside County require mediation for child custody cases?

Yes. Riverside County mandates Child Custody Recommending Counseling (CCRC) when the issue of custody and visitation is before the court. Unlike some other counties, Riverside is a "reporting county," meaning the mediator will make a formal recommendation to the judge if an agreement isn't reached. A skilled Riverside attorney familiar with the CCRC can help navigate this process and obtain a favorable recommendation.

Can I file for an emergency (Ex Parte) order in Riverside?

Yes, if there is an immediate danger or irreparable harm (such as domestic violence or child abduction). Riverside courts have specific morning windows for filing and hearing emergency requests, making it critical to have local counsel ready to act quickly.

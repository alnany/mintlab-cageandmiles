Should You Consider Mediation in Your San Diego Divorce?

Mediation is a confidential and voluntary form of alternative dispute resolution that allows spouses or parents to work toward agreements outside of the courtroom. Unlike litigation, mediation gives parties greater control over outcomes related to custody, support, and property division, while often reducing cost, stress, and time.

At Cage & Miles, mediation is facilitated by highly experienced family law attorneys who are also Certified Family Law Specialists (CFLS). Our mediators focus exclusively on family law dispute resolution and are trained to guide complex negotiations with neutrality, discretion, and legal precision.

Mediation is distinct from court-required custody conferences, such as meetings with Family Court Services in San Diego County. While court services may be mandatory in certain custody cases, private mediation is confidential and not evaluative. The mediator does not make recommendations to the court or impose outcomes. Instead, the process is designed to help parties reach durable, mutually acceptable agreements.

Many families choose mediation to preserve privacy, maintain flexibility, and avoid the uncertainty of litigation. Even when full agreement is not reached, mediation can significantly narrow disputes and streamline the resolution process.

Experienced Military Divorce Lawyers in San Diego

Divorce can be challenging for any family, but military divorces often involve additional legal complexities. In addition to California law, military divorces may be governed by federal laws and military regulations.

The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) provides important protections for active-duty service members, including the ability to postpone divorce proceedings during deployment or when military service interferes with participation in the case. An experienced military divorce attorney can help ensure these protections are properly applied.

Because of San Diego’s large military community and the firm’s own military experience, the attorneys at Cage & Miles have years of experience handling military divorce matters. We understand the unique challenges military families face and work to protect the rights and interests of both service members and their spouses.

Frequently Asked Questions

I want to get divorced. What should I do first?

Divorce is a multi-step process that can be costly and time-consuming. Once you’ve come to the difficult decision to separate from your partner, it’s best to seek out the advice of an experienced family law attorney. These specialists can help you navigate the complexities of the divorce process, facilitate mediation, or represent your interests in the case of a trial. It’s also recommended to find a family law attorney local to your city to ensure they are familiar with the legal landscape and any existing regulations or laws specific to where you live.

How should I choose a family law attorney?

Choosing an attorney is a very personal process. Finding a lawyer who specializes in family law should be a top priority. You’ll also want to choose a lawyer who you feel comfortable with and trust. Reading reviews and asking for referrals from close friends and family can help you narrow down your search. From there, you should schedule a consultation to learn more about the attorney’s and firm’s experiences to ensure it’s a good fit for both parties.

What should I know about divorce in San Diego?

Laws in every state and city vary, so it’s important to know what to expect in your area. California is a no-fault divorce state, meaning that a spouse does not need to exhibit any wrongdoing to get divorced. If this is the case, it should be noted on your initial Petition for Dissolution, which is the first step when filing for divorce in San Diego.

If you bypass mediation and go to trial, San Diego County has a judicial backlog that can cause long wait times of up to a year. Once your trial begins, it will be assigned to one of four San Diego courthouses depending on your zip code. An experienced family law attorney can help you navigate all of these steps, along with helping you prepare for trial and set forth your position.

How long does a divorce take in San Diego County?

In San Diego County, the shortest possible divorce takes six months from the date the divorce petition is served. However, most divorces take longer depending on factors such as child custody, support disputes, property division, and whether the case settles or goes to trial. A San Diego divorce attorney can help streamline the process and avoid unnecessary delays.

Should I consider mediation in my San Diego divorce?

Many divorcing couples choose mediation as a way to resolve disputes privately and outside of court. Mediation allows parties to work with a neutral professional to reach agreements on issues such as custody, support, and property division. Unlike litigation, mediation is confidential and voluntary, and outcomes are controlled by the parties rather than imposed by a judge.

Is divorce mediation confidential in San Diego County?

Private divorce mediation in San Diego County is confidential and conducted outside of the court system. When parties choose voluntary mediation with a neutral, qualified mediator, discussions are protected, and the mediator does not make recommendations to the court.

This process is different from court-required custody conferences through Family Court Services, which may be mandatory in certain custody cases and are not fully confidential. In those situations, if no agreement is reached, the court-appointed counselor in San Diego will provide a recommendation to the judge.

Working with an experienced San Diego family law attorney can help you understand which process applies to your case and determine whether private mediation is an appropriate and effective option.

What should I bring to a consultation with a San Diego divorce attorney?

Bring any relevant documents such as financial records, prenuptial agreements, court papers, and a list of questions or concerns. Providing this information helps your San Diego divorce attorney better evaluate your case and offer clear guidance.

Can I get divorced in San Diego without going to court?

In many cases, yes. If both parties reach agreements through negotiation or mediation, a divorce may be finalized without a formal court appearance. However, court involvement may still be required if disputes arise or if the judge needs additional information. A San Diego divorce lawyer can help you pursue a resolution while minimizing court involvement whenever possible.

What impact does infidelity have on a divorce in San Diego?

California is a no-fault divorce state, which means infidelity generally does not affect whether a divorce is granted. However, infidelity may be relevant in certain situations, such as when marital funds were used to support an affair or when it impacts child custody considerations. A San Diego divorce attorney can help determine whether infidelity has legal significance in your case.
